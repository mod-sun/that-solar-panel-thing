Overview:

To help solve the energy crisis at the consumer level,

 

I'm challenging current restrictive housing code regulation on solar.

 

Our market finds current renewable energy solutions cost-prohibitive.

 

Not only can we produce entry level panels with sustainability in mind,

 

the average user or our team can install them in a non-invasive way.

 

SCROLL

I'm not here to sell you on panels that come out of the box crystal clean, perfectly flat, and not to be touched by anyone but an electrician. No, I don't have a residential license for being tied into the grid, or for lasting a minimum of 25 years, or even to install panels at all. Nbd.

 

I'm proposing an off grid system made with parts that have been available for years, as well as a sustainable, innovative, and hopefully profitable manufacture of "B-Grade" (manufacturer discarded because of imperfections in the surface)

thin-film solar panels, which have proven to be nearly as effective at collecting solar energy, and that's good enough.

 

Through novel encapsulation methods I plan to make flexible solar panels lighter, more affordable, and particularly useful for tracking the sun across the sky, improving solar efficiency considerably through a system of lightweight motors and micro controllers. Version Two will be mounted on a gutter, or as a window mounted flower box without any damage or alteration to the house. An automotive application involves encapsulating cells onto the body of vehicles to improve fuel mileage, enable additional electrical appliances, and heat the car cabin immediately in winter.

 

All versions of the system include a portable, smart rechargeable battery capable which can power household appliances and provide continuous power to the appliances when plugged into the grid if the battery is fully discharged.

 

This is a benefits corporation with a passion for bringing this technology into rural and impoverished areas, sustainable and eco-friendly parts, and enlisting a disenfranchised workforce through grants and cooperation with charities.

 

Approved, licensed, and commercially manufactured solar panels cost around $150 per 100w, I think we can make them for a third of that price, and a tenth of the weight. Best of all, anyone can do it.

 

If you wanted to spend an average of $15,000 on a system that's going to get bolted on to your house forever, there are plenty of clean cut, well-advertised, patented, certified, etc. etc. elsewhere. It's just not my style.

 

I'm here to wage a guerrilla war on the grid. They had us tricked into thinking that power was something only they could handle, but the DIY community is becoming a force that is proving to be too much to regulate, and I'm here to provide a completely open source platform for people to discuss solar innovation at the speed it is developed.

 

 

 

This project is just getting started, but we already have award winning, patentable designs. Since that's the only thing of value that we have so far, it only makes sense to make it available to everyone under no license of any kind, for free.

 

Forum and online purchase capability as well as 3 newly designed working prototypes are in progress.

Expected completion is late May.

Why Free?

I just want everyone to use less non-sustainable energy in whatever capacity they are able. I don't think people understand what happens when you burn oil and coal to power air conditioners. Running the compressor motor and fan ultimately produces much more heat than it removes. It's a very simple thermodynamics  question; what happens when you light a fire in a closed room? We're in a closed room, and we're lighting more fires every day. Natural sources of air scrubbing have long since been completely outpaced by humanity since the industrial revolution. I believe that this tech needs to be promoted by anyone, anywhere, anytime.

 

Open source is the only way to go, and I will be releasing software, design notes, links to where I got the supplies, and instructional videos as the product is deemed safe by experts throughout the design process.

 

If we get crushed by the competition because they can better manufacture products based on my designs,

good.

 

That just means, if that day comes, the right people took control of what should have happened long ago.
